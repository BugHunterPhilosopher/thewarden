Why use Tor?

When sending requests through the internet, there's a risk that your IP address is exposed.

For example, when using a block explorer to confirm a recent Bitcoin transaction, the service running the explorer could potentially link your IP address to someone interested in that Bitcoin address.

Tor uses a routing protocol (onion) that routes traffic through multiple servers and encrypt it each step of the way. It becomes much harder to anyone to identify who is asking for which information.

But privacy comes with some costs...

When using Tor, the routing process will result in slower responses. If you have Tor installed, you can check here how much slower the application requests get.

Boost your privacy

When you run a Samourai Dojo, the blockchain requests are handled by your own node.

This reduces the risk of IP tracking, plus the blockchain download and verification is done locally by your node. The result is much better privacy and reduced risk of accessing a tainted blockchain.

TRUST. BUT VERIFY.


OXT is Samourai's public Block Explorer. We offer users the alternative of using OXT to get and track address information. This option is not ideal. All requests are handled through Tor so IP addresses are not disclosed to Samourai but you are trusting their database.

If their blockchain becomes compromised, you will not know.

We encourage every user to setup a Dojo but for users who are not concerned about the risk of a tainted database, this may be a good compromise.
